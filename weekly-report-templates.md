# Weekly Dive Report — Data Products & Templates

End-of-week deliverables Bret asks for. Produced from the week's logged dive reports
(see `dive-log.md`) + tool predictions + offshore/fish intel + moon phase. Git-tracked so
the format, voice, and past examples are never lost.

## The three products
1. **Facebook post** — the long-form "California Dive Report — Week of [dates]" (full narrative).
2. **Instagram post** — a condensed, punchy version of the same.
3. **Weekly subscriber email** — subject-line options + preheader + region breakdown, signed by Bret.

## Structure & voice

### Regions (always in this order, with a color status emoji)
Color = the week's overall water quality for that region: 🟢 clean/blue · 🟡 moderate/mixed · 🔴 rough/blown out.
- 🟢/🟡/🔴 **SAN DIEGO / LA JOLLA** (Cove, Marine Room, Shores, Point Loma kelp; + offshore: Coronado Is, 9 Mile Bank, the 302)
- **ORANGE COUNTY / LAGUNA** (Shaw's, Treasure Island, Woods Cove, Goff/Crescent, Newport/Pirates Cove)
- **LA / SOUTH BAY** (Palos Verdes, Redondo/Veterans Park)
- **CENTRAL COAST (Carmel / Pt Lobos)** (Monastery, Cypress Pt, Beto's Reef)
- **MONTEREY BAY** (Breakwater/"the wall", McAbee, Mt Chetron, boat pinnacles)

### Recurring content beats
- **Per region:** narrative with specific spots, dates (M/D), viz ranges in ft, water temps in °F, thermocline depth. Bulleted tactical tips. A 🐟 fish/offshore line where relevant.
- **🌡️ Marine Heatwave / oceanography explainer:** adapt to the week's actual pattern — warm murky surface lens over cold clean upwelled water, upwelling driven by NW winds + North Pacific High, blooms greening the surface, "read the whole water column / plan for bottom temp." 
- **🌑 Moon & WSB:** moon phase + white-seabass deep flat-fall/iron bite tie-in.
- **WILDLIFE NOTES:** notable critters (leopard sharks, bat rays, GPO, GSB/black sea bass, sevengill, nudibranchs, etc.).
- **🔗 conditions.spearfactor.com** link + "Dive safe!"
- **Hashtags** on social (e.g. #spearfishing #californiadiving #spearfactor #lajolla #diveconditions #freediving #marineheatwave).

### FACEBOOK — Week of June 22–28, 2026
This week was all about short windows — and the cleanest water hiding down deep. San Diego ripped wide open midweek, with Friday the best La Jolla has looked in months, before a weekend swell and onshore wind knocked it back into the murk. Up in Central California, last week's blue-water run started giving way to green as upwelling reloaded the surface bloom. The throughline on both coasts: a warm, murky surface lens sitting on cold, clean, upwelled water — if you wanted the good stuff, you had to drop through the top.

🟡 SAN DIEGO / LA JOLLA — Peaked Friday, rough finish
A real rollercoaster. Midweek was on the mend (6/24: Cove murky under 10, Shores 10–15, Marine Room 10–15 hazy with fine particulate), then Friday 6/26 went off — La Jolla cleared to the best of the week: Cove 15–20+, Marine Room 20+ over the sand flats, Shores 15–25, gin-clear blue with the reef visible straight through. It didn't hold: by Saturday the surface was hazing back over (water temp dropped to 65°F as upwelling kicked in), and Sunday turned rough — Marine Room down to a murky 7–10 ft on onshore wind, and LJ Shores a surgy 5–10 ft under a long-period 18-second south swell.
- Classic inner-murk / outer-clean gradient again — shallows hazy, clearing as you moved out.
- Big vertical split all week: a warm, murky 65–68°F surface lens over genuinely CLEAN 51–52°F water at depth. At LJ Shores it was 5–15 ft through "greenish gunk" up top but 20–30 ft once you dropped below the 40 ft thermocline; the canyon ran ~10 ft above 50 ft and a clean 20–25 ft below it.
🐟 Fish: Yellowtail were on the La Jolla kelp beds inshore — one speared in the kelp at 20 ft on 6/24 (12–14 ft viz). Offshore, the 9 Mile Bank → the 302 went green nearshore but cleared to 100-ft blue past the bank, giving up two yellowtail to 25 lb on the 302 kelp paddies (6/26). At the Coronado Islands, though, the YT bite was OFF — the islands rolled over cold and green — while white seabass kept biting: two solid fish (33" and 34") landed on flat-falls on 6/26.

🟡 ORANGE COUNTY / LAGUNA — Swell-churned Newport, moderate Laguna
Newport took it on the chin early: Pirates Cove (Corona del Mar) was under 5 ft on 6/24 with 3-foot waves wrapping off the cliffs — a spot you'd hope the harbor would shelter, but it wasn't. Tucked-in pockets at a harbor mouth can actually fish worse in swell, since the waves reflect and churn off the rock. Laguna held up better midweek: Goff Cove ran 8–12 ft on 6/26 with a few clearer pockets to 15.
- In swell, the Laguna rule still applies — Treasure Island, Shaw's, and Woods Cove are your most sheltered bets, and Crescent Bay Park gives you a free look at Seal Rock and Shaw's before you commit.

🟡 LA / SOUTH BAY — Quiet week
Limited reports, but Palos Verdes was running ~10 ft early in the week (6/22).

🟢 CENTRAL COAST (Carmel / Pt Lobos) — Blue window closing
Last week's epic blue water started slipping back toward green as the bloom returned. North Monastery was 10–15 ft and a touch milky on 6/27, and the Pt Lobos coves held a hazy ~20 ft at depth on a flat-calm day (the resident giant Pacific octopus was home at Beto's). A scooter run from Pt Lobos toward Monastery found 20–40 ft at depth — 58°F up top, 52°F on the bottom. The cleanest water was deep; the surface was milking up.

🟢 MONTEREY BAY — Bloom band creeping back
The boat pinnacles still produced at depth, but the mid-water bloom is back. Mt Chetron opened up to 25 ft at the bottom and McAbee ran 10–20 ft with a yellow, snotty algae band stacked from 15–45 ft (6/26). A deep tech dive at Frenchies showed it in cross-section: clear-ish in the top 30 ft, nasty green algae 40–60 ft, then dark-but-clearer below.

🌡️ The Marine Heatwave Split — and what's driving the upwelling
Same heatwave, same lesson: read the whole water column. Surfaces are warm (65–70°F in San Diego, 56–58°F up north) sitting on cold 51–52°F water at depth — and that cold water is active upwelling.
What actually causes it: this time of year the North Pacific High parks offshore and drives persistent northwest winds down the California coast. Those winds, bent by the Earth's rotation, push the surface water offshore — and cold, nutrient-rich water from down deep rises up along the coast to replace it. That's upwelling, and that cold layer you hit at depth is exactly that water. It comes up cold and clear-blue at first, but within a couple of days the nutrients fuel a plankton bloom that greens the surface. So when the NW wind backs off, the bloom stops getting fed and the water can clear (Central CA last week); when it cranks back up, you get cold green water and dropping surface temps — which is what reasserted itself on both coasts this week. Wherever you dove, the cleanest water was below the warm surface lens.

🌑 Moon & WSB
Building toward the full moon (June 29). White seabass kept biting deep on the iron at the Coronados — two landed midweek even with the yellowtail shut off — so the deep flat-fall game stayed alive while the surface bite went quiet.

WILDLIFE NOTES: Bat rays out in big numbers at the Marine Room, with a leopard shark cruising the sand. A giant Pacific octopus holding court at Beto's Reef (Pt Lobos). At depth off LJ Shores: a huge shiner-perch school, young-of-year bocaccio, dozens of ocean whitefish, a yellowfin, and dolphins on the swim-out — plus a kelp perch in the canyon on Sunday. Central CA had blue rockfish, lingcod, and metridium, with salps and jellies drifting through the column.

### WEEKLY EMAIL — Week of June 15–21, 2026
Subject line options (pick one):
- A tale of two coasts: SD soup clears, Central CA turns blue — Dive Report June 15–21
Preheader (preview text): San Diego started in pea soup and finished strong. Central CA flipped to blue water. Same heatwave, opposite results.
Hey divers,
Here's what California waters did this week.
The big theme was a genuine split down the coast. San Diego opened the week in pea soup — a stubborn surface bloom plus King-tide stirring dropped La Jolla to 0–5 ft midweek — then clawed its way back to the best water we'd seen in a while by Sunday. Up in Central California the script flipped entirely: offshore wind scoured the surface bloom out and Carmel/Pt Lobos went full blue-water. Same marine heatwave, opposite results, all driven by wind and coastline.

WILDLIFE NOTES: Leopard sharks moved back onto the Marine Room reef (schooling Saturday and Sunday), with sevengill and tope sightings, and lots of black sea bass.

THE WEEK BY REGION
🔴 SAN DIEGO
- Rough start, strong finish. 6/17 Marine Room to the Cove was 0–5 ft "soup" — a lingering bloom plus King-tide drainage stirring the shallows. White filament streaks still hanging around 5–7 ft on 6/19.
- Recovery came fast: 8–12 ft over sand by Friday, and by Sunday LJ Cove was the best of the week — clean 15–20 ft on the afternoon push.
- Classic inner-murk / outer-clean gradient all week — hazy in the shallows, clearing as you moved out and down.
- Big vertical split: a warm, murky 65–68°F surface lens sitting over genuinely clean 51–52°F water at depth (15–35 ft of visibility once you dropped through it at LJ Shores).
- Point Loma kelp ran 8–20 ft depending on the day. Offshore was the move: Coronado Islands and the 9 Mile Bank both ~30 ft.
🔴 ORANGE COUNTY
A big 18-second south swell (the worst-case direction for these coves) hammered Laguna on 6/16 — Shaw's was 0–5 ft and Treasure Island just 3–4 ft. Slow recovery after: Fisherman's came back to 8–10 ft by 6/18, but Shaw's was still struggling under 10 ft as late as Sunday. In south swell, Treasure Island, Shaw's, and Woods Cove are your most sheltered bets — and Crescent Bay Park gives you a free look at Seal Rock and Shaw's before you commit.
🟡 LA / SOUTH BAY
The South Bay shrugged most of this off. Palos Verdes ran ~15 ft (6/15), the Redondo jetty held 10 ft (6/19), and Veterans Park was in the 14–20 ft range. Expect a sharp thermocline — warm ~65°F up top dropping to 54°F by 25 ft.
🟢 CENTRAL COAST
The week's best story. Offshore wind scoured the surface bloom right out and Central California went clear: Pt Lobos hit 40+ ft of blue water (6/19) and 50+ ft at depth (6/20), Monastery ran 30–35 ft (6/17), and Cypress Point was a solid 30 ft (6/18). Water was a crisp 52°F at depth. If you've been waiting out the summer pea soup up here, this was your window.
🟢 MONTEREY BAY
The bay followed suit. McAbee was 30 ft (6/18 and again 6/20), Breakwater went glassy blue around 20 ft (6/19), and "the wall" held 25 ft. A genuinely nice stretch for Monterey shore diving.

FISH AND OFFSHORE INTEL
- Yellowtail went wide open at the La Jolla kelp beds on 6/17 — a tight early window (roughly 7–9 AM), fish from 7 to 30+ lbs, eating everything. If you want a shot at them shore-adjacent, the dawn window is the play.
- Offshore banks (Coronado Islands, 9 Mile) had small bonito but the yellowtail bite hadn't pushed out there yet — the kelp paddies and Catalina remain the more reliable day-trip targets.

THE MARINE HEATWAVE — READ THE WHOLE WATER COLUMN
The heatwave is still running the show, but the story is in the split:
- Surface: 67–70°F up and down the coast
- At depth: 51–52°F — that cold water is active upwelling
That contrast is exactly what drove the inverse-coast pattern this week. In San Diego the warm surface lens fed the bloom (green and murky up top, clean below). In Central CA the wind mixed it out and gave us blue. Plan for the bottom temp, not the surface — you'll punch through a sharp, cold thermocline either way.

🌑 MOON & KING TIDES
We're coming off the 6/14 new moon, and the King tides early in the week were a real factor down south — extreme tidal swings drained bays and lagoons and stirred the shallows into murk before things settled. White seabass favor the low-light edges around the new moon, so the early-week dawn windows were the smart play.
